About me

"There is nothing pretty much to say about me. I am just a normal girl (or should I say middle aged women since I am in my 30's which all the time makes me cry as I am moving closer and closer to getting old). But Hey! People say Age is just a number right? Atleast there are some saying that makes me feel good about aging too! Anyways I am off the track now. So About me.Ummmm As I said I am just a normal girl who doesnt takes anything the life throws at me in a stride.(atleast I think so). I am happy even for the small things that happens day in and day out. I am a person who thinks there is no point in getting sad over something that we dont have any control. True right!! What else can you do. Either we should be in a position to do something else if we cant do anything,,,, then whats the point in thinking too much about it. So thats me in a nutshell. "

My Family

"I love my family!!! (But then, who doesnt).. These are the people who will stand by you when you feel you are alone in this world. I have a loving husband, a wonderful 8 year old son. (But you wont realize I am so wonderful when I yell at him, which is almost everyday atleast 5 to 10 times). But Hey who can complain about me. Y'all know boys right? Somedays he drives me simple crazy. But then I wouldnt trade him for anyone else in the world. Next comes my husband. He is the total opposite of me. But then opposites attract right!!!! I talk a lot, he seldom talks. I have lot of friends, he has handful of confidente's. I talk on phone for hours and hours he hates talking in the phone. Even now after 10 years of our marriage we still wonder how in heaven we got married. Ours was not at all a love marriage. It was purely arranged by both our parents and I saw him only a day before our parents had planned for our engagement. This is how it happens in India. My mom was a pure terror if she hears the word 'LOVE'. I was scared just because of facing my parents. So just thought that a Love marriage was not my cup of tea. So yeah coming back to my story, I met my husband a day before our engagement day. He didnt know what to talk. Even now he doesnt know what to talk because now I do all the talking..He used to say he never ever realized I will talk this much else he would have reconsidered marrying me. But hey, I was/am a good girl. I dont talk to strangers that much.. Now comes my parents!!! I still am my dad's princess... It doesnt matter how old I am, he will always have my back.. My husband still says that he can never complain about me to my dad and he doesnt have any ally in my side of the family. If he did say something to my dad(I think he has done it before thats y he is able to say this), my dad will tell him his little girl (thats me) might not have meant that or done that etc. See thats what I am talking about my dad always has my back. Be it for my husband. Somedays I tell my husband to learn that from my dad..(My life is settled then!!!). My mom is awesome. She is more stricter than my dad, atleast to me.. My brother was always her pet project. But now when I am a mom with a son, I know how she feels about my brother."

I love cooking too but I will not say it a passion. Its just that I am sometimes simply lazy to get up and cook. "

Place I am from

"I am from India. Typically South India. From a city called Chennai and from state TamilNadu. I love my place. I really do miss my place. Its something in your blood.. Wherever your life takes you, you will still crave for your roots. Thats how it is for me and my husband too.. My son doesnt know any better. We moved here when he was nearly 4years. So he doesnt really remember his Chennai days. There was lot of help back in my place. Both my parents and in-laws where there in Chennai. And know what I didnt have to cook.... Because if I drop in at any of the 2 houses at any meal time, I would be getting food.. OMG how I really miss it.. Here, if I dont cook, we three go hungry... Essentially, I learnt to cook once we koved here.. Other than that there were all sorts of assorted aunts and uncles. So there was never a festival that went without visiting all out relatives place. It was fun. I do miss my INDIA. "
